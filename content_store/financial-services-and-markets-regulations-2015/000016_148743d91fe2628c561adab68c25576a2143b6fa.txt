42.

Exercise of Own-Initiative Power: procedure21

(1)

This section applies to an exercise of the Regulator's Own-Initiative Variation Power or Own-Initiative Requirement Power in relation to an Authorised Person ("A").

(2)

A variation of a Financial Services Permission or the imposition or variation of a requirement takes effect—

(a)

immediately, if the notice given states that that is the case;

(b)

on such date as may be specified in the notice; or

(c)

if no date is specified in the notice, when the matter to which the notice relates is no longer open to review.

(3)

A variation of a Financial Services Permission, or the imposition or variation of a requirement, may be expressed to take effect immediately (or on a specified date) only if the Regulator, having regard to the ground on which it is exercising its Own-Initiative Variation Power or Own-Initiative Requirement Power, reasonably considers that it is necessary for the variation, or the imposition or variation of the requirement, to take effect immediately (or on that date).

(4)

If the Regulator proposes to vary a Financial Services Permission or to impose or vary a requirement, or varies a Financial Services Permission or imposes or varies a requirement, with immediate effect, it must give A written notice.
