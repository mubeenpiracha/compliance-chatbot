Degree of prominence to be given to required indications

Where a communication must, if it is to fall within any provision of this Schedule, be accompanied by an indication of any matter, the indication must be presented to the Recipient—

(a)

in a way that can be easily understood; and

(b)

in such manner as, depending on the means by which the communication is made or directed, is best calculated to bring the matter in question to the attention of the Recipient and to allow him to consider it.

Communications to non-ADGM Recipients and Authorised Persons or Recognised Bodies372

(1)

Subject to sub-paragraph (3), the Financial Promotion Restriction does not apply to any communication which—

(a)

(b)

may reasonably be regarded as being directed (whether from inside or outside the Abu Dhabi Global Market) only at Authorised Persons or Recognised Bodies or persons outside the Abu Dhabi Global Market.

A communication will fall within the exemption in sub-paragraph (1)(b) if the conditions set out in sub-paragraphs (a) to (d) are met. (a)

(d)
