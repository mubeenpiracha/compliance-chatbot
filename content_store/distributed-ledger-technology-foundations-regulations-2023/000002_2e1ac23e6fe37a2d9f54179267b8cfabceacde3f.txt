If the Registrar is not satisfied that a Charter or the White Paper or the Tokenomics Paper or the DLT Framework, if developed, complies with these Regulations, it shall inform the applicant of its decision, together with its reasons within one month of the applicant’s submission of all documents requested by the Registrar.

With effect from the date of registration stated in the certificate of registration, the DLT Foundation shall be a legal entity having separate legal personality and may sue and be sued.

On each anniversary of registration, the Registrar must be paid the annual renewal fee set out in the Distributed Ledger Technology Foundations Regulations (Fees) Rules 2023.

Within one month of the anniversary of the DLT Foundation’s registration, the DLT Foundation shall submit its Confirmation Statement to the Registrar.

Statement of Initial Beneficial Ownership and Control

An application to register the DLT Foundation pursuant to these Regulations must contain a statement of initial beneficial ownership and control, stating who will be considered a Beneficial Owner of the DLT Foundation on registration.

The statement required to be delivered to the Registrar must contain the required particulars and be updated as prescribed in the Beneficial Ownership and Control Regulations 2022.

10.

Registered office

(1)

A DLT Foundation must at all times have a registered office in the ADGM to which all communications and notices may be addressed and which, if the DLT Foundation is required to have a Company Service Provider, may be the same address as that of its Company Service Provider.

(2)

A DLT Foundation may change the address of its registered office by giving notice to the Registrar. The change takes effect upon the notice being registered by the Registrar, but until the end of the period of 14 days beginning with the date on which it is registered, a Person may validly serve any document on the DLT Foundation at the address previously registered.
