Power of authorised Person to require documents etc.

Power of authorised Person to require documents, information and explanations

This section 61 applies where it appears to a Person who is authorised under section 60 that there is, or may be, a question on whether the Annual Accounts or Auditor’s Report complies with the requirements of these Regulations.

The authorised Person may require the DLT Foundation and any Councillor or auditor of the DLT Foundation (existing or past) to produce any document, or to provide him with any information or explanations, that he may reasonably require for the purpose of:

(a)

discovering whether there are grounds for an application to the Court under section 59; or

(b)

deciding whether to make such an application.

If a Person fails to comply with such a requirement, the authorised Person may apply to the Court.

If it appears to the Court that the Person has failed to comply with a requirement under subsection (2), it may order the Person to take such steps as it directs for securing that the documents are produced or the information or explanations are provided.

Nothing in this section 61 compels any Person to disclose documents or information in respect of which a claim to legal professional privilege could be maintained in legal proceedings.

Restrictions on disclosure of information obtained under compulsory powers

This section 62 applies to information (in whatever form) obtained in pursuance of a requirement or order under section 61 that relates to the private affairs of a natural person or to any particular business.

No such information may, during the lifetime of that natural person or so long as that business continues to be carried on, be disclosed without the consent of that natural person or the Person for the time being carrying on that business.

This does not apply:

(a)

to disclosure permitted by section 63; or

(b)

to the disclosure of information that is or has been available to the public from another source.

A Person who discloses information in contravention of this section commits a contravention of these Regulations, if it is in default.

A Person who commits the contravention referred to in subsection (4) shall be liable to a level 3 fine.

For the purposes of subsection (4), a Person is in default unless such Person proves that such Person acted honestly, with Reasonable Skill, Care and Diligence and that in the circumstances in which the activities were carried out, the default was excusable.
