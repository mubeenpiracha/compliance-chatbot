Guidance

Section 2.3 of the RPP Sourcebook sets out matters which the DFSA takes into consideration when making an assessment of the kind under Rule 7.6.3.

7.6.4

In Rule 7.6.3, an individual may not be considered as fit and proper where:

(a)

he is bankrupt;

(b)

he has been convicted of a serious criminal offence; or

(c)

he is incapable, through mental or physical incapacity, of managing his affairs.

7.6.5

In assessing an application for Authorised Individual status, the DFSA may:

(a)

make any enquiries which it considers appropriate, including enquiries independent of the applicant;

(b)

require the individual or Authorised Firm to provide additional information;

(c)

require any information provided by the individual or Authorised Firm to be verified in any way specified by the DFSA; and

(d)

take into account any information which it considers appropriate.

7.6.6 An Authorised Firm must not permit an individual to perform a Licensed Function on its behalf, except as permitted by section 11.6, unless that individual is an Authorised Individual who has been assessed by the Authorised Firm as competent to perform that Licensed Function in accordance with Rule 7.6.7.
