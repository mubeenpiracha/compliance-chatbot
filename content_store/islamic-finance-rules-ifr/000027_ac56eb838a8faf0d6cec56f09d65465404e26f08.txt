ISLAMIC FINANCE RULES (IFR)

Article 13 of the Law Regulating Islamic Financial Business 2004 requires an Authorised Person undertaking Islamic Financial Business to appoint a Shari’a Supervisory Board.

When an Authorised Person appoints a Shari’a Supervisory Board, it must ensure that:

(a)

the Shari’a Supervisory Board has at least three members;

(b)

the members appointed to the Shari’a Supervisory Board are competent to perform their functions as Shari’a Supervisory Board members;

(c)

any appointments, dismissals or changes in respect of members of the Shari’a Supervisory Board are approved by the Governing Body of the Authorised Person; and

(d)

no member of the Shari’a Supervisory Board is a director or Controller of the Authorised Person.
