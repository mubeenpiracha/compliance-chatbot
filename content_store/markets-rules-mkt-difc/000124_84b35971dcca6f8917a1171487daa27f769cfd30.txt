Guidance

1.

A Person is regarded as exercising significant influence over a Reporting Entity, for example, if that Person is a consultant or adviser to the Reporting Entity.

2.

Any transactions between a Subsidiary of a Reporting Entity and a Related Party is included within the definition of a Related Party Transaction. This is because a Related Party may, through the Reporting Entity, be able to influence terms which are more favourable to the Related Party when transacting with the subsidiary. .Such transactions could be detrimental to the interests of the Reporting Entity.
