6.

The Insurer should have in place procedures to insulate the Insurer, so far as practicable, from potentially adverse effects of Group activities (for example, transfer pricing or fronting) or Group events that may expose the Insurer to risk. Such procedures could include requirements for transactions with Group members to be at arm’s length, and for maintenance of ‘Chinese walls’, and development of contingency plans.
